Chapter 1Project overview

The Trading Bot at a glance

An autonomous, paper-trading bot that runs three independent strategies — multi-lane US equity, 24/7 spot crypto, and an options-wheel — under a single orchestrator, decision store, and risk envelope.

Every order passes a layered gate stack (data quality, compliance, intel, risk caps, VaR, daily/weekly P&L circuit-breakers) before reaching the broker.

A nightly research lab runs purged walk-forward backtests, gates promotions on PBO and Deflated Sharpe, and uses a Claude Opus 4.7 architect/reviewer pipeline to draft new strategy templates.

Operational tooling — diagnose, daily digest, midday snapshot, dashboard, alert drain — keeps the human in the loop without coupling decisions to the human's response time.

Why three workflows in one process? Each workflow has its own data cadence (1500-stock universe vs 24/7 crypto vs 50-symbol option chains), but they share the same orchestrator, DecisionStore, RiskManager, and Alpaca paper account. The shared envelope is what enforces gross/net caps and per-strategy halts across all three at once.
Chapter 2System architecture

End-to-end data & control flow

The bot is structured as a small set of long-lived processes (the trading daemon, the lab, the dashboard, and a supervisor) sharing a SQLite state database, an append-only DecisionStore, and a single Alpaca paper account. Every workflow flows through the same orchestrator and risk envelope.

Process layout

Four launchd plists in ops/launchd/ bring up the system:

  • daemon — the live scheduler (APScheduler) that runs every cron job in §12.
  • lab — independent scheduler for the research loop (§9).
  • dashboard — FastAPI UI that reads state.db read-only.
  • supervisor — restarts any of the above on watchdog failure (watchdog_account.py, watchdog_stall.py).
Why one orchestrator for three workflows? All three live workflows share RiskManager and DecisionStore, which means a per-strategy halt or a global gross/net cap applies across them in one place. TradeOrchestrator.scan reads the asset class from the candidate and picks the right gate path, but the envelope is shared.

W1 · Stocks momentum / multi-lane equity

The flagship US-equity workflow. Pre-market builds the universe and ranks 1500+ tickers; through the day, scanners run multi-lane signal generation, intel gates, and risk checks before submitting bracket orders to Alpaca paper.

CLI trigger
bot intel-scan • bot full-run • bot rich-report
Schedule
stock_scanner (CronTrigger every N min, 09:00–15:00 ET) · premarket_rank (07:30 ET) · massive_refresh (06:30 ET) · midday_rerank (12:00 ET)
Asset class
US equities
LLM in path?
Not in entry path — entries are deterministic indicators. LLM enters only at the lab/evolution layer (StrategyArchitect, CodeReviewer, Calibrator) on Saturdays via roles/strategy_architect.py.

End-to-end sequence

  1. 06:30 ET — massive_refresh refreshes Polygon grouped bars and the active universe.
  2. 07:30 ET — premarket_rank re-scores candidates and writes strategy/opportunities.md.
  3. Every N min, 09:00–15:00 ET — stock_scanner job calls bot intel-scan.
  4. _load_active_universe pulls top-25 stocks from the ranked watchlist.
  5. Stage-1 screener (screener.build_stage1_shortlist) computes 1d / 5d / volume score relative to SPY.
  6. Stage-2 lanes (Momentum / MeanReversion / Breakout) emit RankedCandidate with conviction.
  7. Live regime detection via FRED VIX (regime.detect_regime).
  8. TradeOrchestrator.scan iterates per symbol:
  9. W2b restricted-list (compliance.check_restricted) → skip if restricted
  10. Idempotency check (existing position / pending order / already-traded-today)
  11. Bar fetch + MIN_BARS_FOR_INDICATORS
  12. W2a data-quality (check_bar_freshness, check_completeness)
  13. MomentumStrategy.evaluate emits Signal with stop_loss + take_profit
  14. News-sentiment floor (news_sentiment.passes_filter)
  15. _news_intel_gate runs stock_earnings_gate, stock_insider_cluster_gate, macro_shock_gate
  16. RiskManager.check enforces per-trade-risk %, max-position %, concentration, asset-class regime cap, gross/net cap, daily/weekly halt, per-strategy halt
  17. AlpacaClient.place_order_with_stop_loss submits a bracket order.
  18. TradeJournal.append + RiskAfter populated.
  19. DecisionStore.append persists the audit, compliance, data-quality, alerts, execution-constraints JSON columns.
  20. 16:05 + 21:55 ET — Reconciler reconciles fills against Alpaca account.
  21. 16:30 ET — daily_digest email summarises trades, P&L, and rejections.

Chapter 5 Workflow W3 — Options-Wheel (CSP → assigned → CC) Live

W3 · Options-Wheel (CSP → assigned → CC)

A premium-selling wheel that sells cash-secured puts on quality names, holds the stock when assigned, then sells covered calls at strike ≥ cost basis. State-machine driven, no LLM.

CLI trigger
bot wheel-scan • bot wheel-manage • bot wheel-close • bot iv-capture • bot wheel-universe-build • bot wheel-status
Schedule
wheel_universe_build (21:30 ET nightly) · iv_capture (09:45 ET weekdays) · wheel_scan (10:15 ET weekdays — single daily entry) · wheel_manage (every 30 min 10:00–15:00 ET weekdays)
Asset class
Equity options
LLM in path?
None — fully deterministic. LLM only via shared lab/evolution layer.

End-to-end sequence

  1. 21:30 ET nightly — wheel_universe_builder walks Alpaca optionable equities, applies allowlist + Finnhub mcap/listing-age filters, writes wheel_universe_cache.
  2. 09:45 ET weekdays — iv_capture mass-fetches option chains, writes option_iv_history rows.
  3. 10:15 ET weekdays — wheel_scan runs once for the day.
  4. Per symbol gather: regime, VIX, sentiment_score, IV rank, ApeWisdom WSB spike, Finnhub earnings, current WheelCycle row.
  5. WheelLane.passes_preflight: wheel enabled; cycle phase ∈ {None, assigned}; regime ∈ {trending_up, sideways}; VIX in [floor, ceiling]; sentiment ≥ floor; IV rank ≥ 30; no WSB spike; no earnings within dte_max+2 days.
  6. Stage-3 contract pick: pick_csp_contract or pick_cc_contract by delta band (typ. 0.20–0.30) and DTE band.
  7. Risk gates: option_collateral_ok (allocation cap + per-symbol concentration); _sector_cap_check (default 25%).
  8. OptionAlpacaClient.sell_to_open at limit-bid; cycle state recorded via wheel_state.open_csp / open_cc.
  9. Every 30 min 10:00–15:00 ET — wheel_manage iterates active cycles:
  10. Take-profit at mid ≤ (1 − take_profit_pct) × credit
  11. Force-close at dte ≤ dte_force_close
  12. Roll if |delta| ≥ delta_breach AND rolls_used < max_rolls_per_cycle

  • Polygon news sentiment (preflight floor)
  • option_iv_history table (IV rank)

  • WheelLane: Phase.NONE → CSP_OPEN → ASSIGNED → CC_OPEN → CLOSED

W4 · News-driven LLM trader

A discretionary news-event trader powered by Claude Opus 4.7. Detects events with regex, packages context for the LLM, and parses a strict JSON action — currently library-only, not yet wired into the scheduler or CLI.

CLI trigger

Asset class

LLM in path?
Yes — pinned to claude-opus-4-7 at news_trader/news_controller.py:38.

End-to-end sequence

  1. Caller assembles a NewsControllerInput (symbol, market state, position, recent news cluster, primary-filing flag, novelty score).
  2. event_extractor.extract_event runs pure-regex classification — no LLM call yet.
  3. news_controller.propose_news_trade builds the system prompt (NEWS_CONTROLLER_PROMPT_VERSION = 'v1').
  4. AnthropicClient.complete is called with model='claude-opus-4-7'.
  5. Strict JSON output is parsed; on parse error → fail-closed Decision(action='no_trade').

Outputs

Chapter 7News & intelligence sources

Every feed the bot consumes

The bot's intelligence layer is split across a baseline aggregator (intelligence.py), a per-trade gate framework (intel_gates.py), a Polygon REST client (massive_client.py), and dedicated single-source modules. Compliance and data-quality checks fail closed; intel filters fail open by design — when a free feed is degraded, the trader continues with a logged warning rather than seizing up.

fail-open — If the feed errors or returns no data, the gate passes — the orchestrator does not block trading on a degraded free feed.
fail-closed — If the feed errors, the gate blocks — used for data-quality and compliance only.

Chapter 9The Lab — research & auto-promotion

Walk-forward, PBO, Deflated Sharpe, and an LLM architect

The Lab (src/trading_bot/lab.py) is a long-running launchd daemon that reproduces and stress-tests strategies on historical data. Its goal is to push only statistically meaningful changes into the live config — and to refuse promotions that look good in-sample but fail honest out-of-sample tests.

Components

Purged walk-forward

src/trading_bot/walkforward.py   ·   entry: default_folds, walk_forward_backtest

  • Default 6 folds — 12-month train + 3-month test, walking by test_months=3.

Probability of Backtest Overfitting (PBO)

src/trading_bot/validation/pbo.py   ·   entry: probability_of_backtest_overfitting

  • Implements Bailey & López de Prado 2014 CSCV (Combinatorially Symmetric Cross-Validation).
  • n_partitions = 10 → C(10, 5) = 252 IS/OOS combinations.
  • For each combination, picks the IS-Sharpe argmax and tests whether its OOS rank > N/2.
  • Returns the fraction of combinations where the IS-best is below median OOS — promotion gate: PBO > 0.5 BLOCKS.

Deflated Sharpe Ratio (DSR)

src/trading_bot/validation/dsr.py   ·   entry: deflated_sharpe_ratio

  • Implements Bailey & López de Prado 2014 — penalises Sharpe by the number of trials and the higher moments of returns.
  • sharpe_max_estimate = (1 − γ)·Φ⁻¹(1 − 1/N) + γ·Φ⁻¹(1 − 1/(N·e)) with γ ≈ 0.5772 (Euler-Mascheroni).
  • z = (SR_obs − SR_max) · √(n−1) / √(1 − skew·SR + (kurt−1)/4 · SR²) → return Φ(z).
  • Pure-Python normal CDF / inv-CDF — no scipy dependency. Promotion gate: DSR > 0.95.

Optuna TPE evolution

src/trading_bot/evolution.py + roles/param_optimizer.py   ·   entry: optimize_strategy

  • optuna.create_study(direction='maximize', sampler=TPESampler()) — n_trials=100, 4-hour SLA budget per run.
  • Search space (param_space.py): rsi_lower (50–60), rsi_upper (65–75), ema_period (15–30 int), stop_pct (3.0–7.0), sentiment_floor (−1.0–0.0).
  • Each trial calls BacktestEngineerRole over a 31-month window with n_folds=6.
  • Fitness = compute_fitness(alpha_vs_spy_x, sortino, max_dd_pct) — combines all three into one scalar.

Calibration drift

src/trading_bot/calibration.py   ·   entry: compute_drift_score

  • Spearman rank correlation (Pearson on ranks) between predicted edge and realised P&L.
  • Severity bands: > 0.5 ok · 0.3–0.5 warning · < 0.3 high → halt Promoter for 7 days.
  • Below 10 samples → 'insufficient_data', no halt.

VaR engine — historical, additive, deliberately conservative

VaR is the conservative tail used in pre-trade and post-trade risk reporting. The implementation is deliberately additive — no correlation credit — so the limit always overestimates risk rather than understates it.

FunctionFileBehaviour
historical_var(returns, alpha=0.05)var_engine.py:17Magnitude of the bottom-α empirical quantile loss; 95 % default.
expected_shortfall(returns, alpha=0.025)var_engine.py:41Mean of the worst α tail (CVaR). Reported alongside VaR.
var_after_trade(positions, candidate)var_engine.py:62Adds the candidate's marginal VaR to the portfolio total — no correlation netting.
Layered halts Three independent kill switches act at different scopes: per-strategy halt (other lanes keep trading), daily/weekly P&L circuit breaker (whole portfolio), and LLM cost halt (refuses any Anthropic call when monthly spend hits 100%). The last one protects the lab from runaway loops.
